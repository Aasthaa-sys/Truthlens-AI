**1. Facial Anomalies & Inconsistencies:**

*   **Unnatural Blinking:** Deepfakes often struggle with natural blinking patterns. The eyes might blink too frequently, too slowly, or at irregular
intervals.
*   **Skin Tone/Texture Issues:** Deepfake faces often have odd skin tones, textures that look unnatural (too smooth, too waxy), or slight color

*   **Shading Problems:** The shadows on a deepfake face frequently don’t align properly with the light source in the image. There might be strange
highlights or areas where shadows are missing.
*   **Mouth Movement & Lip Sync Errors:** Deepfakes frequently misinterpret speech, leading to unnatural mouth movements that don't match what the
person is supposedly saying. The lips may not line up perfectly with the audio.
*   **Pupil Reflections:**  The reflections in the pupils are often wrong, inconsistent with the lighting, or missing altogether.

**2. Artifacts & Digital Glitches:**

*   **Blurry or Pixelated Areas:** Deepfakes sometimes introduce blurring or pixelation around edges or in areas where different images have been
blended together.
*   **Color Discrepancies:** Colors might not blend smoothly between the original image and manipulated sections, creating banding or color shifts.
*   **Strange Noise Patterns:** Digital noise can appear in unexpected places – often along edges or in areas with complex textures.

**3. Contextual Issues & Lack of Consistency:**

*   **Inconsistent Lighting:** The lighting within the image may not match other photos or videos of the person, or it could simply be unrealistic.
*   **Unrealistic Expressions:**  The facial expression might look forced, unnatural, or out of character for the person in question. Deepfakes can
struggle to accurately reproduce subtle emotional nuances.
*   **Lack of Background Details:** In some cases, the background details are poor or inconsistent, suggesting they were added later to try and mask
flaws.

**4. Technical Signs (Difficult to Detect Without Expertise):**

*   **Compression Artifacts:**  Deepfakes can introduce unique compression artifacts that aren’t typically found in genuine images.
*   **Metadata Issues:** The image's metadata might be missing, altered, or contain information inconsistent with the source. (However, this is easily
manipulated).



**How Deepfake Detection Works - and why it's difficult:**

Deepfake detection methods are constantly evolving. They typically involve:

*   **AI-Powered Analysis:**  Sophisticated AI algorithms analyze subtle inconsistencies that humans might miss.
*   **Facial Landmark Tracking:**  Tracking the movement of key facial landmarks (eyes, mouth, nose) to identify unnatural movements or blending
errors.
*   **Frequency Analysis:** Examining the frequency patterns in an image for anomalies introduced during manipulation.
